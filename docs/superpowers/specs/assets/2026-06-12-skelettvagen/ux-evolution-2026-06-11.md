## Full catalogue

### 1. Persona chip + one-tap quick-swap tray in the composer — 9.00

- **Target:** `persona-wear-per-channel` · **Lens:** friction · **Cost:** M
- **Wave fit:** M5 skeleton — design as the new composer's identity slot; works as a M5+ drop-in on the old bones too.
- **Scores:** owner-values 8 · feasibility 9 · player 10

**Idea:** Replace the toolbar <select> with a persona CHIP docked at the composer's left edge: 44px round avatar (or monogram) of the currently-worn persona, name tinted in its palette color. The chip is the always-visible answer to 'who am I speaking as' — today that's invisible outside the opt-in preview. Tap → a glass quick-swap tray (bottom-sheet idiom on mobile, anchored popover on desktop): a horizontal row of recently-worn personas for THIS channel plus account-wide most-used, MRU persisted to localStorage exactly like the color-swatch quick-swap (move-to-front, dedup, cap 6, unit-tested pure fn — the pattern already exists in act/compose_colors.rs); last slot is 'Wardrobe…' opening the full modal. Tapping a face fires the existing channel_active_persona PATCH and dismisses — 2 taps total vs today's 3-4-tap modal round trip. Long-press the chip = instant toggle to the PREVIOUS worn persona (the alt-tab of mid-scene two-character play), no tray at all. Build it as a standalone composer-module component so it rides whichever Skelettvågen composer wins (orb / command strip / HUD bar all need a persona surface).

**Why better:** The catalogue names this 'the biggest interaction gap on the surface' for a personas-first RP app. This collapses the most frequent roleplay-central action from a modal expedition to one or two thumb taps, makes worn identity permanently visible, and reuses an existing endpoint + an existing tested MRU pattern. Server-side nothing changes — posting.rs already re-checks can_edit_persona at send.

**Owner-value fit:** Mobile-first thumb economy; immersion (faces, not form controls); persona-tint palette finally used at the composing surface; identical UX both pointer classes.

**Risk:** Low — pure client over existing endpoints; only design risk is composer row crowding, which the chip actually reduces by deleting the <select>.

**Judges:**

- *owner-values* (8): Pragmatic and invariant-clean — reuses a tested MRU pattern and the existing wear endpoint; long-press previous-persona is pure mid-scene two-character thinking.
- *feasibility* (9): Verified: the endpoint exists and the MRU move-to-front/dedup/cap fn is already written and unit-tested in compose_colors.rs — this is near-pure assembly of proven parts, honest M trending S, zero invariant contact.
- *player* (10): Long-press = alt-tab between my two characters mid-scene — the single biggest friction in the app dies, with zero new gesture risk and identity finally visible while I write.

### 2. Self-healing sync: SSE resurrection + sync truth on mobile — 8.67

- **Target:** `sse-realtime-backbone` · **Lens:** friction · **Cost:** S
- **Wave fit:** Standalone QoL batch — pure act/sync.rs work, fully skeleton-independent.
- **Scores:** owner-values 9 · feasibility 9 · player 8

**Idea:** Replace forever-per-session poll fallback with a recovery loop in act/sync.rs: after handover to polling, re-attempt EventSource with exponential backoff (30s doubling to a 5-min cap) AND immediately on visibilitychange→visible and online events — the two moments a phone actually regains network. A successful open swaps back to SSE seamlessly (poll loop stops, Ghost Quill resurrects — today it silently dies forever after one bad minute of cell coverage). Surface the truth on mobile without topbar real estate: the channel-name trigger's ▾ caret doubles as the sync dot (mint glow = LIVE, dim = polling — 0 extra px), and tapping the desktop chip or long-pressing the mobile trigger opens a small glass popover: plain-language state, 'Reconnect now' button, and push-subscription status (subscribed / not subscribed / unsupported here) — the catalogue's 'no in-app indicator of push state' fixed in the same popover.

**Why better:** A backgrounded PWA currently degrades to 1.5s polling FOREVER and tells mobile users nothing — degraded sync is hidden exactly where it matters most (mobile networks), and Ghost Quill 'silently doesn't exist'. Reconnect-on-foreground matches real phone lifecycle; the id-only bus invariant is untouched (this changes only when the client listens, never what rides the bus).

**Owner-value fit:** Honesty-first status reaching the meta platform at last; battery-friendlier than eternal polling; zero new chrome.

**Risk:** Backoff must not thrash on flaky networks (cap + jitter); the chip-never-lies rule (set on onopen, dropped on onerror) must be preserved through the retry states.

**Judges:**

- *owner-values* (9): The honest, battery-kind fix at the exact moments phones regain network, zero chrome consumed, Ghost Quill resurrects — maximal value per line at S cost.
- *feasibility* (9): Backoff + visibilitychange/online retry is exactly how phone radios actually behave, it's contained to act/sync.rs, costs ~S, touches nothing on the bus, and fixes a forever-degraded state — best value-per-line in the friction set.
- *player* (8): Ghost Quill silently dying forever after one bad minute of cell coverage means degraded sessions I never even knew about — reconnect-on-foreground fixes the real phone lifecycle.

### 3. Ghost Quill: ember decay, in-channel seance invitation, markup ghosts, poll parity — 8.33

- **Target:** `ghost-quill` · **Lens:** presence · **Cost:** M
- **Wave fit:** standalone QoL batch
- **Scores:** owner-values 9 · feasibility 8 · player 8

**Idea:** Four coordinated fixes. (a) Ember decay: the act layer keeps the last permission-fetched draft per author; on TTL expiry or empty fetch it marks the row .ghost-ember (opacity sinking toward 0.2 over ~30s, mono 'paused' tag) instead of deleting — a fresh fetch rehydrates it. Thinking pauses read as thought, not flicker; the server's honest 8s TTL is untouched. (b) Invitation: when the typing line renders and the VIEWER's receiver pref is off, append a 44px quill chip; tapping opens a small consent sheet (states exactly what is shared, that it is mutual) and flips the pref — plus optionally emits a targeted id-only nudge so the partner sees 'X lit their ghost quill — light yours?' (fetched from a tiny membership-gated GET). The opt-in escapes the account-modal burial without weakening double-consent. (c) Render ghost drafts through the panic-free markup parser exactly like the own-draft preview — co-writers drafting colored dialogue stop previewing as markup soup. (d) Poll parity: when the receiver pref is on, the legacy poll loop fetches /typing-drafts at its 1.5s cadence, so the feature stops silently not-existing under POLLING. Draft text still flows ONLY through the permission-checked GET; the bus stays id-only throughout.

**Why better:** Every catalogued Ghost Quill pain (buried opt-in, vanish-flicker, raw-markup ghosts, silent death under polling) is addressed without touching the TTL/mutex/bus invariants the feature was praised for shipping inside. It graduates the wave's riskiest feature from tech demo to the co-writing presence ritual it was meant to be.

**Owner-value fit:** Consent stays double and explicit (recorded privacy recommendation); markup ghosts honor the parser invariant; poll parity is UX-equality for bad networks — the floor device on mobile data is exactly who falls back to polling.

**Risk:** Ember shows up-to-30s-stale text — it was legitimately fetched, but the 'paused' label must be unmistakable so nobody mistakes an ember for a live draft. Poll-parity adds one request per 1.5s tick for opted-in viewers; acceptable at friend-group scale, gate it on the pref.

**Judges:**

- *owner-values* (9): Every Ghost Quill pain fixed inside the TTL/mutex/bus invariants, and poll parity is UX-equality for exactly the floor-device-on-bad-network user — the séance done right.
- *feasibility* (8): Four fixes that each reuse an existing mechanism (class+transition, pref flip, panic-free parser, gated poll fetch) without touching TTL/mutex/bus — M honest; the stale-ember labeling and per-1.5s fetch cost are correctly bounded at friend-group scale.
- *player* (8): Markup-parsed ghosts and poll parity mean the séance exists on the couch's bad wifi instead of only in demos — every recorded pain of the feature we love, fixed.

### 4. Corridor hum: live quill marks on channel rows, fed by events you already receive — 8.33

- **Target:** `new` · **Lens:** presence · **Cost:** S
- **Wave fit:** standalone QoL batch
- **Scores:** owner-values 8 · feasibility 9 · player 8

**Idea:** Typing { channel_id } SSE events already arrive per-connection for ALL visible channels — the client just ignores non-open ones (deliberately, to avoid refetch storms). Without ANY new fetch, set an ephemeral per-channel 'someone writes here now' flag (8s decay, mirroring the typing TTL) in a shell-level signal map and render a tiny animated quill-stroke or single twinkling star on that channel's row in the sidebar/sheet, plus a faint shimmer on its guild circle. **Risk:** A busy guild could make the channel list twinkle distractingly — cap to one mark per row, no counts, and make it the same quiet visual weight as the hearth ember layer.

**Judges:**

- *owner-values* (8): Inhabitedness conjured from events the client already discards — zero new requests, reveals strictly less than the typing line; the lens's highest delight-per-line.
- *feasibility* (9): Converts events the client already receives and discards into ambient signal for literally zero added requests; an 8s-decay signal map + one quiet mark is honest S and the graceful absence under polling is already the house pattern.
- *player* (8): Hearing pens scratch behind doors for literally zero added requests — I'd wander into live scenes I'd otherwise miss entirely; highest delight-per-line in the pile.

### 5. Channels as adjacent rooms: finger-following swipe strip with momentum snap — 8.00

**Idea:** Replace tap-only channel switching with Koncept A's pane strip: the open channel becomes the middle pane of a 3-pane virtualized horizontal strip (prev/next channels from sidebar order). Neighbor panes lazily fetch their first page through the existing permission-checked list_messages on first peek, but last-seen/mark-read advances ONLY when a pane settles as current for >=300ms (peeking never wipes unread — same discipline as the sheet's select-only guild taps). **Why better:** The catalogue names channel switching as the highest-frequency action, and today every switch is a modal trip (Servers tab → sheet → tap). **Judges:**

- *owner-values* (10): The Koncept-A signature gesture itself: thumb-meta, desktop drags the identical strip, kills the rejected blink-cut warp, floor-cheap transforms, read-discipline preserved — every binding value at once.
- *feasibility* (5): Buildable (Telegram-class pattern, touch-action:pan-y + pointer math works in WASM) but it's the top of L: 3 mounted ChannelPanes on a 3GB POCO, SSE open-channel semantics rework, and an axis-lock that must arbitrate against swipe-to-reply, radial, and sheet — weeks of real-device tuning are the true cost.

### 6. The Outbox: optimistic send with idempotent retry — no message ever lost to a tunnel — 8.00

- **Target:** `mobile-qol-pass` · **Lens:** friction · **Cost:** L
- **Wave fit:** M5 skeleton — this IS one of the 'new bones' (the composer/send pipeline of any winning skeleton should be built outbox-first).
- **Scores:** owner-values 9 · feasibility 6 · player 9

**Idea:** Promote sending to an outbox state machine. Client: send enqueues an OutboxEntry {client_nonce (random 16 bytes), channel, body, persona, effect, reply_to, media_ids} into a signal persisted to localStorage (the drafts pattern), renders it INSTANTLY at the list tail with the established ghost-row styling plus a thin progress shimmer, and posts it; success swaps ghost→real on the next reconcile (matched by nonce echoed in the response), failure flips the row to a 'Retry / Discard' pair and a quiet offline pip when navigator.onLine is false. Background: pending entries auto-retry on online/visibilitychange with backoff. Server: SendMessageRequest gains optional client_nonce; a UNIQUE index on (channel, author, client_nonce) makes the retry idempotent via the existing with_write_conflict_retry → on conflict return the already-created message (the codified 409/idempotent house pattern, retry.rs) — a double-tap or flaky-network resend can never double-post. Rolls keep their own path (immutable, server-RNG) and are excluded from the outbox.

**Why better:** Today a send on a failing mobile network ends as red text in the status line — the composing work is hostage to the radio. Optimistic send + durable retry is the single biggest daily-feel upgrade for the actual usage pattern (phones, couches, commutes), and the catalogue already argues M12's daily-feel items belong in the new bones, not at the tail. The idempotency design reuses the app's existing write-conflict discipline rather than inventing one.

**Owner-value fit:** Mobile-meta resilience; anticipates failure instead of reporting it; invariants reinforced (identity from cookie, content never on the bus — outbox is purely client+REST).

**Risk:** The nonce column is a new field on a populated table — MUST follow the option<>/backfill rule (NONE-coercion gotcha) and ride a single migration; ghost/outbox rows must stay out of cursor/seen bookkeeping like all sentinels.

**Judges:**

- *owner-values* (9): The deepest mobile-meta resilience win: composed words never held hostage by the radio, idempotency via the house 409 pattern, rolls properly excluded — invariants reinforced.
- *feasibility* (6): Right long-term design and L is honest, but there's an undisclosed landmine: a UNIQUE index over (channel, author, client_nonce) on a populated table where every old row has nonce=NONE may refuse to build — needs backfill-with-random or index strategy work, plus nonce must ride MSG_PROJECTION for ghost-vs-SSE dedup; verify before committing the wave.
- *player* (9): Losing a 40-minute prose post to a network blip is the single worst thing that happens in mobile RP — this ends it forever.

## Blind spots (completeness critic)

Areas the whole pass — inventory AND proposals — missed entirely. None of these are scored; they are candidates for the next lens pass or for direct triage.

1. @-mention surface, entirely: the server resolves mentions into pinged_users (/Users/damien/Developer/authlyn-interactive/src/server/messages/posting.rs resolve_mentions) and emoji got an autocomplete (channel/emoji_suggest.rs), but there is no @-name autocomplete in the composer, no feedback when a typed mention fails to resolve, and no distinct mention-badge/highlight treatment vs generic unread — neither inventory nor proposals mention mentions once.
2. Full-text message-history search: no search endpoint exists anywhere in src/server/messages/ — 'memory-core-search' covers lore, but finding 'that scene from March' across a long-form RP archive (the thing this app accumulates) has no UI, no endpoint, and no proposal.
3. Blocking and abuse reporting: friendship.state only allows 'pending'/'accepted' (/Users/damien/Developer/authlyn-interactive/src/storage/schema.surql:268) — no block state, no per-user mute, no report-this-message path (the feedback table is app feedback, not abuse reports). Safety tooling is a complete blank in both lists.
4. The invitee side of joining a guild: invite_member adds a user by exact username instantly (src/server/guilds/membership.rs) — no accept/decline consent, no 'you were added to X' notification moment, and no shareable invite links for inviting anyone whose handle you don't already know. The join experience exists in code but is uninventoried on both ends.
5. Account lifecycle UX: registration-form ergonomics (autocomplete attributes for password managers, iOS strong-password suggestion, show-password toggle), the security-question public reset flow's actual experience (src/server/auth/password.rs exists but is never inventoried), session-expiry mid-write (does a 401 bounce to /login eat the composer draft?), and account deletion / export-my-story are all absent.
6. Screen-reader experience of the live stream: zero aria-live regions in the codebase — incoming messages, roll results, typing indicators, and the status-line toast (which 'Transmissions' restyles but never announces) are silent to SR users; both lists cover modal/sheet a11y but not the core chat surface, and attachments have no alt-text affordance.
7. The a11y cost of the zoom lock: 'pwa-viewport-zoom-lock' ships user-scalable=no, but no item or proposal offers compensation — no in-app text-size setting, no honoring of OS font scaling / Dynamic Type. Low-vision users lose pinch-zoom and get nothing back.
8. prefers-contrast / forced-colors: reduced-motion is a catalogued strategy, but grep finds no prefers-contrast or forced-colors handling in style/ — and nobody examines whether user-picked persona colors stay legible against the dark nebula-glass chrome (no contrast validation on the color swatch picker).
9. Link UX beyond parsing: markup autolinks bare URLs (src/markup/tokenize.rs Link token), but there are no link previews/unfurls, no external-link affordance or rel/safety story, and no decision about how links open from installed-PWA standalone mode (in-app webview vs Safari) — links never appear in either list.
10. Empty states: brand-new account with zero guilds, freshly created channel with zero messages, empty friends pane, empty lorebook — 'The Commons' is about liveness and 'Boarding sequence' about first-run, but the blank-canvas states a new group actually stares at are designed nowhere.
11. Server-down and error-page states: route-level 404, the health-check swap window during a fenrir deploy, and a full server restart mid-session have no user-facing story — 'Self-healing sync' covers SSE reconnection mechanics, not a 'station is dark' maintenance state or what a cold page load shows when the backend is gone.
12. The failure half of media upload: staging and client-side downscale are covered, but upload progress on a large file over mobile data (64 MiB limit), cancel mid-flight, retry-on-failure, and the rejection messaging for over-limit or non-image files (someone WILL try a video) are unmentioned.
13. Offline reading: public/sw.js precaches the shell and an offline page, and 'The Outbox' covers send-side, but cached scrollback — re-reading last night's scene in airplane mode — and the actual content/quality of that offline fallback page are in neither list.
14. Tablet/iPad middle ground: inventory covers phone (bottom tabs) and desktop (three-column reskin) but nothing addresses the tablet breakpoint, hybrid touch+pointer input, or iPad split-view widths — an in-between class the mobile-first/desktop-degraded doctrine never names.
15. Landscape phone as a layout: style/ only handles landscape safe-area insets (_layout.scss, _nav.scss); landscape as an experience — keyboard consuming most of the viewport, composer ergonomics, message-list density — is never treated as a surface.
16. Desktop keyboard QoL: no quick-switcher (Cmd+K), no Esc/arrow-key navigation of messages or panes, no shortcut reference — every proposal is touch-first, and under the owner's own UX-equality value, keyboard users have no parity item at all.
17. Composer platform text behavior: autocorrect/autocapitalize/spellcheck attributes are never discussed — iOS autocorrect actively mangles invented RP names, and the lorebook is sitting right there as a custom-dictionary/no-correct source. Nothing in either list touches it.
18. Copy and share-out of message text: the recent iOS selection-suppression fix (b853ef8) removed native text selection, but no copy-message-text action exists in the radial/action-row, and there are no message permalinks / copy-link-to-message — 'golden-lines' makes quote cards, plain copying is orphaned.
19. Composer length-limit feedback: MAX_BODY_CHARS is enforced server-side (posting.rs:58 rejects oversize), but there is no character counter or near-limit warning — long-form RP writers compose a wall of prose and only learn the limit from a post-hoc error.
20. PWA icon badging: unread/mention count on the installed home-screen icon (Badging API) — push lifecycle and The Herald are catalogued, but the app-icon badge, the thing you see before opening anything, is absent from both lists.
